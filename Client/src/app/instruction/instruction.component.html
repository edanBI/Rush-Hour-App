Instructions - Rush-hour

In this task you will play a version of the game Rush-hour.

Rush Hour is a sliding block puzzle invented by Nob Yoshigahara in the 1970s.

The goal of the game is to get only the red car out through the exit of the board by moving the other vehicles out of its way.
However, the cars and trucks obstruct the path which makes the puzzle harder.

The board is a 6x6 grid, The game comes with 16 vehicles (12 cars, 4 trucks), each colored differently.
Cars and trucks are both one square wide, but cars are two squares long and trucks are three squares long.
Vehicles can only be moved along a straight line on the grid; rotation is forbidden. v

There are 3 levels to the game, etch level contains questions on the level, You can't preside the game until all the questions bean anserd.
You will have 10 minutes to solve etch level.
You may use the UNDO feature to undo your immediately preceding move,
The RESET the feature will restore the car and track to their first positions, at the start of the level.

You will have the opportunity to get a bonus based on your performance - if all of your solutions will be in the right range of corectness, you get a bonus of 2.5$.
